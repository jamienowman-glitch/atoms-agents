## 2. The New Bottleneck: "Context Drift"
However, the previous assumption that "Agents makes it easy" is false. The bottleneck has shifted from **Labour** to **Context Management**.

### The Fight Against Entropy
The biggest challenge for the AI-Native founder is not syntax, but **Drift**.
*   **The Problem**: As the codebase grows (400+ modules), agents lose the "North Star". They hallucinate imports, forget architectural constraints, and overwrite working logic with generic boilerplate.
*   **The Reality**: The founder is not just an "Idea Guy"; they are a **Shepherd**. They must constantly re-align the swarm, clean the context, and force-feed the architectural vision back into the system. It is a daily battle against the agents' tendency to revert to the mean.

## 3. The Role of the "Architect-Founder"
This requires a new type of founder. They don't need to know how to write a generic `useEffect`, but they must possess:
1.  **Systemic Vision**: Understanding *what* connects to *what* (Architecture).
2.  **Radical Articulation**: The ability to describe a feature so precisely that a non-deterministic agent cannot misunderstand it.
3.  **Tolerance for Chaos**: Managing a team that works at lightspeed but has the memory span of a goldfish.
